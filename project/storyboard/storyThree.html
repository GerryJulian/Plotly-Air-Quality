Government Efforts

Air pollution remains one of the world’s most pressing environmental challenges — but nations are responding with bold action.

The United States, China, India, Mexico, and Brazil have each launched ambitious programs to improve air quality through cleaner transport, stricter regulations, and green investments. Together, their efforts showcase diverse strategies driving the global transition toward cleaner air.

Congestion Mitigation & Air Quality Program (CMAQ)

Established under the Clean Air Act, the Congestion Mitigation and Air Quality (CMAQ) program is a U.S. federal initiative that provides funding for transportation projects designed to reduce harmful emissions and improve air quality in densely populated regions.

Project scope includes:

  • Development of public transit infrastructure
  • Installation of electric vehicle (EV) charging stations and alternative fuel facilities
  • Promotion of advanced vehicle technologies and alternative fuel adoption
  • Enhancement of multi-modal access through bicycle and pedestrian lanes
  • Support for shared micromobility initiatives such as e-bikes and scooters

Source: U.S. Department of Transportation

Beijing-Tianjin-Hebei Air Pollution Control Program

Driven by a comprehensive, multi-sector investment strategy, this program aims to reduce air pollutants and carbon emissions at their sources. Its initiatives focus on enhancing energy efficiency, expanding clean energy adoption, and strengthening air pollution controls. The program covers industrial modernization as well as transport infrastructure improvements.

To date, the total investment in this program amounts to US$1.3 billion, jointly contributed by the World Bank, Huaxia Commercial Bank, and its sub-borrowers.

Source: World Bank Group

National Clean Air Program (NCAP)

Launched in 2019, the NCAP aims to improve India’s air quality by cutting particulate matter (PM) levels by 20%–30% by 2024, later extended to 2026 with an increased target of 40%. A major initiative under NCAP was the installation of Continuous Ambient Air Quality Monitoring Stations across 130 cities to better identify pollution hotspots and guide targeted mitigation measures.

However, poor air quality in India stems from multiple sources, not just vehicle emissions. As shown in the pie chart, vehicle-related initiatives accounted for only 14% of NCAP’s 2024 funding, revealing a disproportionate allocation that underscores the program’s lack of a holistic approach.

Source: Centre for Research on Energy and Clean Air

Mexico City's ProAire Program

First launched in 1995, the ProAire Program remains an active cornerstone of Mexico City’s air-quality strategy. Over the years, it has achieved several notable milestones, including:

  • Implementation of a weekly “Hoy No Circula” (“No-Drive Day”) to reduce vehicle emissions
  • Introduction of a low-emission Metrobus system to improve public transport efficiency
  • Efforts to make the city less car-centric by expanding bike lanes and public bicycle stations
  • Promotion of hybrid and electric taxis as cleaner mobility alternatives

Source: Centre for Public Impact - Founded by BCG

Brazil's National Policy on Air Quality (Law Passed in May 2024)

The new policy establishes a unified national air quality monitoring system across Brazil to safeguard public health and the environment. Its provisions include:

  • Creation of the National Air Quality Information System
  • Tax incentives for companies adopting cleaner technologies and sustainable practices
  • Public education initiatives to raise awareness about air quality and pollution impacts on health
  • Establishment of a national air pollution mitigation fund, financed through fines and penalties imposed on environmental violators

Source: Mayer Brown Publications
